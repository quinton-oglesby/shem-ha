Federal Cartel Office allows Rosneft to buy large stake in German refinery
The government is trying to be tough on Moscow with sanctions. Earlier this week, however, the Federal Cartel Office still cleared the purchase of large shares in a German refinery by Russian state-owned Rosneft.
Feb. 24, 2022, 6:40 p.m.
Many cars in Germany run on fuel from the PCK refinery in Schwedt - and the Brandenburg-based company could soon be firmly in Moscow's hands, despite current political tensions. The German Federal Cartel Office gave Russian state-owned Rosneft antitrust approval for the takeover of major company shares as recently as Monday, Feb. 21, 2022.


The approval just three days before Russian troops invaded Ukraine was granted because the purchase did not raise any competition concerns, a spokesman for the Bonn-based agency said. Merger control is considered independently of geopolitics, he said. Rosneft's planned purchase of a further 37.5 percent of the shares could thus soon further expand the Russian business with German gasoline.

Acquisition applied for on January 20
Specifically, the company, in which gas lobbyist and former Chancellor Gerhard Schr√∂der is chairman of the supervisory board, should hold 91.67 percent of PCK Raffinerie after the acquisition, according to the company itself. Competitor Shell had offered the shares, Rosneft exercised its right of first refusal as an existing shareholder - and subsequently applied for the acquisition of the additional shares on January 20, according to the Cartel Office.

The antitrust approval of the deal comes at a time when the German government is actually trying to sanction Russia in terms of economic policy. Whether the sale can still be stopped is questionable. At least potentially conceivable is a so-called investment review by the Federal Ministry of Economics. Most recently, the takeover of Siltronic by a Taiwanese group failed in the course of such a lengthy review - the ministry allowed the deadline for a clearance certificate to expire.

It is questionable whether Economics Minister Robert Habeck (Greens) will also have such an examination carried out in the case of Rosneft's plans, or whether he will be able to have it carried out. SPIEGEL has submitted a corresponding inquiry to the ministry. The review is about the question of whether the foreign investment is expected to impair public order or security for the Federal Republic

Brandenburg's Minister President Dietmar Woidke had praised Rosneft's planned greater involvement in PCK back in November, with the SPD politician calling it "good news."

More than 1,100 people work for PCK in Schwedt, where they produce diesel, gasoline or heating oil. The location is strategically chosen: An important pipeline from Russia ends there, which, according to the refinery, accounts for 12 million tons annually, or 25 percent of Germany's crude oil demand. Its name is "Druzhba", which means "friendship".
